One scatter plot and two datasets

The scatterplot shows the number of prostitution crimes versus vehicle theft crimes for all the police districts of San Francisco. Each point corresponds to a district and its radius is proportional to the total amount of crimes for the chosen period. If one hovers over a point, the name of the district as well as the total crime count is shown. One can toggle between data from 2003 and 2015 by clicking the two buttons below.

The ranges of the axes are stable and based on the two datasets as a whole. The reason for this is for it to be easier to grasp the developemnt and/or differences between the two data sets. If the axes were dynamic, the maximum for both years would visually be placed the same place because the range of the axes would have been changed.

Visualizing geodata

The map visualizes the results of the K-means algorithm with k = 2..6 that has been run on the San Francisco crime data. One can toggle between the results by clicking one of the 5 buttons. By hovering above one of the buttons, a preview of that result will be shown on the map (have a little patience while hovering, it's not super fast). The centroids of each of the results are indicated by black rings.
